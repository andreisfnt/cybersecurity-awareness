## Purpose

This self-assessment is for IT leaders who want an honest picture of their organization's current security maturity - not to produce a compliance report, but to identify where investment and effort will have the most impact.

It is designed to be completed by someone with direct knowledge of the IT environment, not filled in aspirationally.

---

## Maturity scale

Use the following scale for each domain:

| Level | Label | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | Initial | No defined process. Activity is ad hoc or reactive. |
| 2 | Developing | Some processes exist but are inconsistent, undocumented, or not reliably followed. |
| 3 | Defined | Processes are documented, consistently applied, and understood by the team. |
| 4 | Managed | Processes are measured, monitored, and actively improved. Gaps are identified and addressed. |
| 5 | Optimized | Processes are continuously improved based on data, incidents, and threat intelligence. Security is embedded in organizational culture and decision-making. |

Be honest. Level 3 is a strong operational baseline for most organizations. Most organizations are somewhere between 2 and 3 across most domains.

## Prioritization guide

Use this to turn assessment results into a focused improvement plan.

**Start with the highest-impact gaps, not the lowest scores.** A level 1 in backup and recovery is more urgent than a level 2 in network segmentation for most organizations.

High-priority areas to address first:
- Any level 1 in access control, endpoint security, backup and recovery, or incident response
- MFA not enforced for email and remote access
- No incident response process
- No backup testing
- No data classification or handling policy

Medium-priority areas:
- Inconsistent patching
- Third-party access not reviewed
- Security awareness training not completing

Lower-priority but important for maturity progression:
- Monitoring and detection capability
- Network segmentation
- Data loss prevention
