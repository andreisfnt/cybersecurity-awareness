## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes rules for the secure and appropriate use of organizational email and communication platforms. It protects the organization from legal liability, data exposure, and reputational harm arising from misuse of communication systems, and sets clear expectations for all users.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, and third parties using organizational communication systems
- All organizational communication platforms: email, instant messaging, video conferencing, and collaboration tools
- Use of organizational communication systems on any device, including personal devices

---

## 3. Ownership and privacy

Organizational communication systems are provided for business purposes. Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using them.

[Organization name] owns all messages, files, and data created, sent, received, or stored on organizational communication systems. This includes messages sent from organizational accounts on personal devices.

[Organization name] reserves the right to monitor, log, audit, and review use of organizational communication systems at any time, in accordance with applicable law.

---

## 4. Email use

### 4.1 Acceptable use

Organizational email accounts are provided for business communication. Incidental personal use is permitted where it does not:

- Interfere with work performance
- Violate any part of this policy
- Expose the organization to legal or reputational risk

### 4.2 Professional conduct

All email communication sent from an organizational account must be professional in tone and content. Employees are representing the organization in all external communications.

### 4.3 Confidential information in email

- Email is not a secure channel for transmitting highly sensitive or regulated data unless encrypted
- Sensitive information should not be included in the body of an email where an alternative secure method is available
- Employees must verify recipient addresses before sending sensitive information - misdirected emails are one of the most common causes of data breaches
- External forwarding of sensitive organizational data is prohibited without authorization

### 4.4 Email retention

Organizational email is retained in accordance with the organization's data retention policy. Employees must not delete email in an attempt to circumvent retention obligations or in anticipation of legal proceedings.

### 4.5 Email forwarding

- Automatic forwarding of organizational email to personal accounts is prohibited
- Manual forwarding of individual emails to personal accounts for work purposes is only permitted for non-sensitive content
- Mass forwarding of email to personal accounts is prohibited under all circumstances

### 4.6 Shared and functional mailboxes

Access to shared or functional mailboxes must be requested through IT and approved by the relevant business owner. Access is reviewed periodically and removed when no longer required.

## 5. Phishing and suspicious email

### 5.1 Recognizing suspicious email

Employees should treat any email with caution if it:

- Creates urgency or pressure to act quickly without verification
- Requests credentials, payment, or sensitive information
- Contains unexpected attachments, particularly from unknown senders
- Has a sender address that does not match the claimed identity
- Contains links to websites that do not match the claimed destination
- Is unexpected, even if apparently from a known sender

### 5.2 What to do with suspicious email

- Do not click links or open attachments in suspicious emails
- Report the email to IT immediately via [reporting method - e.g., report phishing button, helpdesk]
- Do not forward suspicious email to colleagues
- Do not attempt to reply to or engage with suspected phishing senders
- If you have already clicked a link or opened an attachment in a suspicious email, report it to IT immediately - do not wait

Clicking a phishing link is not a disciplinary matter if reported promptly. Failing to report it is.

### 5.3 Business email compromise

Be alert to requests that arrive by email asking for:

- Urgent fund transfers or changes to payment details
- Changes to supplier bank account details
- HR data or employee personal information
- Credentials or access to systems

Verify any such request through a separate, known communication channel before acting. Call the person directly using a number you already have - do not use contact details provided in the suspicious email.

## 6. Instant messaging and collaboration platforms

The following principles apply to all organizational messaging and collaboration platforms:

- Use platforms approved and provisioned by IT
- Do not use personal accounts on organizational collaboration platforms for work purposes
- Do not share sensitive organizational data in channels or workspaces visible beyond the intended audience
- Treat written communication in messaging tools with the same professionalism as email - messages are logged and may be reviewed
- Do not connect unauthorized third-party applications or integrations to organizational collaboration platforms without IT approval

---

## 7. Video conferencing

- Use organizational video conferencing accounts provisioned by IT, not personal accounts, for work-related meetings
- Apply appropriate access controls to meetings containing sensitive discussions (waiting rooms, passwords, host controls)
- Be aware of physical surroundings when participating in video calls from non-office locations - visible screens, documents, or conversations within earshot are a data exposure risk
- Record meetings only where there is a legitimate need and participants have been informed

---

## 8. Prohibited use

The following uses of organizational communication systems are prohibited:

- Sending or forwarding content that is offensive, discriminatory, harassing, threatening, or obscene
- Sending or forwarding confidential information without authorization
- Impersonating another individual or organization
- Sending unsolicited bulk email (spam) from organizational accounts
- Knowingly sending or forwarding messages containing malware or malicious links
- Using organizational communication systems for personal commercial activities
- Using communication systems to circumvent other organizational policies

---

## 9. Email signature

Employees communicating externally should use a consistent, organizationally approved email signature that includes:

- Full name and job title
- [Any legally required disclaimers - consult legal if uncertain]

Do not include personal social media accounts, personal contact details, or unofficial branding in organizational email signatures.

---

## 10. Leavers

When an employee leaves the organization:

- Organizational email accounts are disabled upon departure
- Access to collaboration platforms is revoked upon departure
- Business-critical email or messages may be retained and transferred to a relevant colleague in accordance with the data retention policy
- Auto-reply messages are set up for a defined period where business continuity requires it
