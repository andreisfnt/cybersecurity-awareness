## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes [Organization name]'s commitment to protecting the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of its information and IT systems. It defines the responsibilities, principles, and minimum controls that apply across the organization.

This is the overarching information security policy. More detailed policies and procedures exist for specific domains (access control, data classification, incident response, acceptable use) and should be read alongside this document.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All information assets owned, processed, or held by [Organization name], regardless of format or location
- All employees, contractors, and third parties with access to organizational systems or data
- All IT systems, networks, devices, and applications used for organizational purposes
- All locations where organizational work is conducted, including remote and home working environments

---

## 3. Information security principles

[Organization name] manages information security according to the following principles:

**Confidentiality** - information is accessed only by those with a legitimate business need and appropriate authorization.

**Integrity** - information is accurate, complete, and protected from unauthorized modification.

**Availability** - information and systems are accessible to authorized users when needed for business purposes.

**Proportionality** - security controls are proportionate to the risk and value of the information being protected. Not all information requires the same level of protection.

**Accountability** - responsibilities for information security are clearly assigned and enforced.

---

## 4. Roles and responsibilities

### Senior leadership

- Approves this policy and allocates resources for information security
- Sets the tone for security culture across the organization
- Reviews significant security risks and accepts or directs mitigation

### IT / Security function

- Owns, maintains, and implements this policy and supporting controls
- Manages security incidents and coordinates response
- Conducts risk assessments and recommends controls
- Provides security awareness training and guidance to employees
- Reports on security posture to leadership

### Managers

- Ensure team members read, acknowledge, and comply with security policies
- Escalate security incidents and concerns to IT promptly
- Participate in access reviews for their teams
- Support the security awareness program

### All employees

- Comply with this policy and all supporting security policies
- Complete required security awareness training
- Report security incidents, suspected incidents, and concerns promptly
- Take personal responsibility for the security of devices and credentials in their care

### Third parties

Third parties with access to organizational systems or data are required to comply with security requirements as defined in their contracts and applicable data processing agreements.

---

## 5. Risk management

[Organization name] manages information security risk through a risk-based approach:

- Security risks are identified, assessed, and prioritized based on likelihood and potential impact
- Risk treatment decisions (mitigate, accept, transfer, avoid) are made at an appropriate level of authority
- Residual risks are documented and reviewed at least annually
- New projects, systems, and significant changes are assessed for security risk before implementation

The IT function is responsible for maintaining a risk register and ensuring material risks are escalated to leadership.

---

## 6. Asset management

- All significant IT assets (devices, systems, software) are recorded in an asset register
- Assets are assigned a responsible owner
- Assets are classified according to their sensitivity and criticality
- Assets are disposed of securely - data is wiped or destroyed before disposal or reuse

---

## 7. Access control

- Access to systems and data is granted on the principle of least privilege - minimum access necessary for the role
- Access is provisioned through a documented request and approval process
- Access is reviewed periodically and revoked promptly when no longer required
- Privileged access (administrative access) is separately managed, logged, and reviewed
- Shared accounts and shared credentials are not permitted except where technically unavoidable, and must be documented
- Multi-factor authentication is required for all systems where it is technically available, with priority on remote access, email, and systems containing sensitive data

---

## 8. Data classification and handling

[Organization name] classifies information into the following categories:

| Classification | Description | Examples |
|----------------|-------------|---------|
| Public | Information intended for external audiences | Published website content, public reports |
| Internal | General business information not intended for public release | Internal communications, operational procedures |
| Confidential | Sensitive business, personnel, or commercial information | HR records, financial data, contracts, strategic plans |
| Restricted | Highly sensitive information with legal, regulatory, or significant business risk | Personal data subject to GDPR, regulated data, credentials |

Employees are responsible for classifying information correctly and handling it according to the requirements of its classification. The Data Classification Policy provides detailed handling requirements for each level.

---

## 9. Cryptography

- Sensitive data must be encrypted at rest and in transit
- Encryption standards in use must meet recognized current standards
- Cryptographic keys are managed and protected - access to keys is restricted and logged
- The IT function is responsible for maintaining approved cryptography standards

---

## 10. Physical and environmental security

- Access to areas containing sensitive IT equipment or information is restricted to authorized personnel
- Visitors to restricted areas are accompanied
- Sensitive documents are not left unattended on desks or in shared spaces
- Clear desk and clear screen practices are expected of all employees
- Physical media containing sensitive data is stored securely and disposed of appropriately

---

## 11. Supplier and third-party security

- Third parties with access to organizational systems or data are subject to security due diligence before engagement
- Security requirements are included in contracts with relevant third parties
- Third-party access is limited to the minimum necessary for the service provided
- Third-party access is reviewed and revoked when the relationship ends
- Material security incidents involving third parties are reported and managed under the incident response process

---

## 12. Incident management

- All employees are required to report security incidents and suspected incidents promptly
- The IT function is responsible for incident detection, triage, response, and recovery
- Significant incidents are escalated to senior leadership
- Incidents involving personal data are managed in accordance with GDPR notification obligations
- Post-incident reviews are conducted for significant incidents to identify improvements

The Incident Response Playbook provides detailed procedures for managing security incidents.

---

## 13. Business continuity

- Critical systems and data are backed up regularly, with backups stored separately from primary systems
- Recovery procedures are documented and tested
- Business continuity plans identify the IT systems and data critical to organizational operations and define recovery priorities
- Recovery capability is tested at least annually

---

## 14. Compliance

[Organization name] is required to comply with applicable laws, regulations, and contractual obligations relating to information security. These include but may not be limited to:

- General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and applicable national data protection law
- [Sector-specific regulatory requirements, if applicable]
- Contractual security obligations with customers, partners, and suppliers

The IT function is responsible for monitoring the regulatory environment and ensuring policies remain compliant.

---

## 15. Security awareness and training

- All employees complete security awareness training upon joining and annually thereafter
- Training covers the threats most relevant to the organization and the employee's role
- Completion of required training is tracked and followed up
- Additional targeted training is provided following incidents or identified capability gaps

---

## 16. Policy compliance and exceptions

All employees are required to comply with this policy. Exceptions require documented approval from the IT owner or CISO and must include a defined review date.

Non-compliance may result in disciplinary action proportionate to the nature and impact of the violation.

---

## 17. Policy review

This policy is reviewed annually or following a material change to the organization, its risk environment, or applicable regulation. The IT owner is responsible for initiating and completing the review.
