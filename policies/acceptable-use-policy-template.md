## 1. Purpose

This policy defines the acceptable use of IT resources provided by [Organization name]. It exists to protect the organization, its employees, its data, and its reputation - and to ensure that IT resources are available and functional for legitimate business purposes.

This policy applies to everyone who uses organizational IT resources. Uncertainty about whether an action is permitted is itself a reason to ask before proceeding.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, consultants, and temporary staff
- All IT resources owned, leased, or operated by [Organization name], including devices, networks, applications, and data
- Personal devices used to access organizational systems or data (BYOD)
- Use of organizational resources while traveling, working remotely, or working outside normal hours

---

## 3. Acceptable use

### 3.1 General principles

IT resources are provided for business purposes. Incidental personal use is permitted where it does not:

- Interfere with job performance or organizational operations
- Consume significant organizational resources (bandwidth, storage, processing)
- violate any part of this policy or applicable law
- Create risk for the organization or its data

### 3.2 Devices

Employees are expected to:

- Keep devices physically secure - do not leave devices unattended in public or unsecured locations
- Lock the screen when stepping away, even briefly
- Report lost or stolen devices to IT immediately
- Not allow family members, friends, or other unauthorized individuals to use organizational devices
- Not attempt to circumvent or disable security controls, endpoint protection, or device management

### 3.3 Network and internet

Acceptable network and internet use includes:

- Business research and communication
- Accessing systems and data required for job functions
- Incidental personal use that does not violate this policy

### 3.4 Email and communication

- Use organizational email for business communication
- Apply professional judgment to all written communication - organizational email and messaging are not private
- Do not use organizational email to sign up for personal services unrelated to work

### 3.5 Data and files

- Store work data in organizational systems, not personal storage accounts
- Apply the organization's data classification policy when handling, sharing, or storing information
- Do not transfer sensitive organizational data to personal devices or accounts without authorization

---

## 4. Prohibited use

The following are prohibited under all circumstances:

### 4.1 Legal violations

- Any use of IT resources that violates applicable law, including copyright infringement, fraud, harassment, or unauthorized access to third-party systems
- Accessing, storing, or distributing illegal content of any kind

### 4.2 Security violations

- Attempting to bypass, disable, or interfere with security controls, monitoring systems, or access restrictions
- Installing unauthorized software, including tools that could be used for network scanning, password cracking, or exploitation
- Sharing credentials, access cards, or authentication tokens with other individuals
- Accessing systems or data beyond your authorized scope, even if access controls do not prevent it
- Connecting unauthorized devices to the organizational network

### 4.3 Data misuse

- Accessing, copying, or transmitting organizational data without authorization
- Sending sensitive organizational data to personal email accounts or personal cloud storage
- Sharing confidential information with third parties without authorization
- Using organizational data for personal benefit

### 4.4 Harmful or inappropriate content

- Creating, accessing, storing, or distributing content that is offensive, discriminatory, harassing, or threatening
- Using organizational resources to conduct personal business ventures
- Engaging in cryptocurrency mining or similar resource-intensive personal activities on organizational systems

### 4.5 Misrepresentation

- Impersonating another user, employee, or third party
- Falsifying identity or authorization to gain access to systems or data

---

## 5. Privacy and monitoring

Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using organizational IT resources. [Organization name] reserves the right to monitor, log, and review use of organizational systems, networks, and devices in accordance with applicable law.

Monitoring may include:

- Network traffic and internet access logs
- Email and communication metadata
- Device activity logs
- Access logs for organizational systems

Monitoring is conducted for security and compliance purposes. Employees will be notified of monitoring practices through this policy and applicable employment documentation.

---

## 6. Personal devices (BYOD)

Employees using personal devices to access organizational systems or data must:

- Ensure the device meets the organization's minimum security baseline (encryption enabled, screen lock, up-to-date OS)
- Enroll the device in organizational device management if required for the access level needed
- Accept that IT may remotely wipe organizational data from a personal device in the event of loss, theft, or departure from the organization
- Not store organizational data locally on a personal device beyond what is necessary for immediate use

---

## 7. Remote working

All provisions of this policy apply when working remotely. Additional considerations:

- Use a secure, private network connection. Avoid conducting sensitive work on public Wi-Fi without VPN
- Be aware of physical surroundings when handling sensitive information - screens visible in public spaces are a data exposure risk
- Do not print sensitive organizational documents to home or public printers unless authorized

---

## 8. Reporting obligations

Employees must report to IT immediately if they:

- Lose or have a device stolen
- Suspect their credentials have been compromised
- Receive a suspicious communication that may be a phishing attempt
- Inadvertently access, send, or expose data they should not have
- Observe behavior by others that appears to violate this policy

Reports should be made to [IT helpdesk / security contact] via [contact method]. Early reporting of potential incidents significantly reduces their impact.

---

## 9. Violations

Violations of this policy may result in:

- Suspension or revocation of IT access
- Disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment
- Legal action where violations involve criminal conduct or significant harm to the organization

The severity of the response will be proportionate to the nature and impact of the violation.
