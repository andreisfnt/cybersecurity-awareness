## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the use of personally owned devices to access [Organization name] systems, data, and networks. It defines the conditions under which personal devices may be used for work purposes and the obligations that apply to employees who choose to do so.

BYOD is a privilege, not a right. Access can be revoked if the conditions of this policy are not met.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All employees, contractors, and third parties who use personally owned devices (smartphones, tablets, laptops) to access organizational systems or data
- All personally owned devices regardless of operating system or form factor
- Access to organizational email, calendars, collaboration platforms, file storage, VPN, or any other organizational system from a personal device

Employees who do not use personal devices for work purposes are not subject to this policy.

## 4. Minimum security requirements

A personal device used for work purposes must meet all of the following requirements at all times:

- [ ] Operating system is current and actively supported by the vendor
- [ ] Operating system and applications receive and install security updates promptly
- [ ] Device is protected with a PIN, password, or biometric lock with auto-lock after [5 minutes] of inactivity
- [ ] Full-device encryption is enabled
- [ ] No jailbreaking or rooting - devices that have been jailbroken or rooted are not permitted
- [ ] A reputable security or antivirus application is installed where applicable to the platform
- [ ] The device is not shared with other individuals for work use

IT reserves the right to verify compliance with these requirements and to deny or revoke access for devices that do not meet them.

---

## 5. Acceptable use on personal devices

### 5.1 Permitted use

Employees using personal devices for work may:

- Access organizational email, calendar, and contacts
- Use approved collaboration and communication platforms
- Access approved cloud-based work applications
- Connect to organizational VPN where required for their role (subject to IT approval)

### 5.2 Prohibited use on personal devices

- Storing organizational data in personal cloud storage accounts (personal Dropbox, Google Drive, iCloud, etc.)
- Transferring organizational data to personal applications or accounts
- Accessing systems beyond the scope approved for BYOD access
- Using a personal device on a jailbroken or rooted operating system
- Allowing family members or others to use the device while it has active access to organizational systems

---

## 6. Data handling on personal devices

- Organizational data accessed on personal devices must not be stored locally beyond what is required for immediate use
- Sensitive or confidential organizational data must not be downloaded or cached on personal devices unless explicitly permitted for the role
- Employees must not back up organizational data to personal cloud services
- Personal and work data should be kept as separate as possible - use dedicated work profiles or containers where available on the platform

---

## 7. Organizational rights and remote management

By enrolling a personal device under this policy, the employee acknowledges that [Organization name] has the right to:

- Remotely remove organizational data and applications from the device (selective wipe)
- Enforce security policies on the organizational data container or profile (password requirements, encryption)
- Monitor access to organizational systems from the device
- Perform a full remote wipe of the device in circumstances where selective wipe is technically unavailable and the risk to organizational data is significant (this action will only be taken after all alternatives have been exhausted and with appropriate authorization)

**Important:** A full remote wipe will erase all data on the device, including personal data. Employees should maintain personal backups. IT will notify the employee before performing a full wipe wherever circumstances allow.

---

## 8. Lost or stolen devices

Employees must report a lost or stolen personal device to IT **immediately** - within [2 hours] of becoming aware of the loss.

Prompt reporting is critical. IT will initiate remote data removal upon report. Delays in reporting increase the risk of unauthorized access to organizational systems and data.

To report: [IT helpdesk / emergency contact / number]

---

## 9. Support

[Organization name] IT provides support for organizational applications and data on personal devices. IT does not provide support for:

- Personal applications or data on the device
- Hardware faults or physical damage to the device
- Operating system issues unrelated to organizational access
- Costs of personal plans, data, or repairs

---

## 10. On departure or policy termination

When an employee leaves the organization, changes role, or BYOD access is otherwise terminated:

- IT will remove all organizational data and applications from the enrolled device
- The employee must confirm removal or submit the device for verification if requested
- IT will provide advance notice of the removal process where possible
- Organizational data held on personal storage (downloads, screenshots, exports) must be deleted by the employee

---

## 11. Monitoring and privacy

[Organization name] monitoring applies to organizational data and applications on the device, not to personal data or personal applications. Specifically:

- IT can monitor access logs for organizational systems accessed from the device
- IT can enforce and verify policy compliance on the organizational profile or container
- IT does not monitor personal calls, messages, photos, or applications on the device
- Employees should be aware that use of organizational applications on a personal device creates access logs

---

## 12. Acknowledgment

By enrolling a personal device, the employee confirms they have read, understood, and agree to comply with this policy, and that they understand the organizational rights described in Section 7.
